--- layout: post title: What's New in the Lab description: Over the past month, Vanadium has released two new products, and has another on the way. date: '2010-02-17T11:53:00.009-05:00' author: Michael Ferguson tags: - 3d-printing - arbotix - robots modified_time: '2010-03-26T00:36:08.210-04:00' thumbnail: http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_qmthA_fNJ-Y/S3wgafjmaxI/AAAAAAAAAJE/SZrTuweZM_w/s72-c/makerbot.jpg blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-8995060597167955455.post-1333041004996495265 blogger_orig_url: http://www.showusyoursensors.com/2010/02/whats-new-in-lab.html --- It's been a busy month, and the lack of updates here is a testament to that. Over the past month, Vanadium has released two new products, and has another on the way. The MINI RoboController has been on shelves for a few weeks at Trossen, and the ArbotiX Commander should be in stock later this week.

We also purchased a MakerBot, which is now up and running. This little guy will be producing robot parts soon, as I have a number of projects on the backlog that I want to get going.

As for that product on the way: how about controlling some RX-64 servos with your ArbotiX? You'll be able to do just that with our new RX-Bridge, a little add-on board for the ArbotiX that converts it from an AX-12 controller, to an RX-series controller (and EX-106's too).

Lastly, we've been hard at working testing, and the V1.1 release of PyPose/NUKE is just around the corner. Should be out very shortly, with a number of improvements and bug-fixes. Unfortunately, mammal-style IK will be held over until v1.2.
